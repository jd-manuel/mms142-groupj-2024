  2. SWOT ANALYSIS

This SWOT analysis evaluates the current state of e-wallets, in the rapidly evolving digital payment landscape. By examining factors such as ease of use, financial inclusion, cybersecurity challenges, and global expansion potential, it highlights both the advantages and obstacles in driving adoption and innovation. This comprehensive overview provides insights into strategic directions for enhancing user trust, accessibility, and technological integration.

Strengths

  • Ease of use

    • E-wallets are easy to navigate and get used to, particularly leading brand GCash (INQUIRER.net, 2024), allowing users to register, transact, and access online financial services with shorter learning curves and fewer technical complications.

  • Financial inclusion

    • Through Philippine Identification System (PhilSys)-assisted know-your-customer (KYC) procedures, e-wallet service providers offer quick registration to individuals and micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSMEs), including those who are previously unbanked—meaning, those who could not create and, thus, did not own bank accounts due to lack of or misplaced identity files. (BSP, n.d.)

  • Integration into services

    • E-wallets have embedded themselves in ecosystems that support modern living, including those catering to users’ everyday needs such as e-commerce, ride-hailing, and food delivery.

  • Regulatory and Policy Support

    • Through the Digital Payments Transformation Roadmap 2020–2023, BSP has created an enabling regulatory environment to support the growth of digital innovations, including e-wallets.

  • High Smartphone and Internet Usage

    • Since 2018, the average number of mobile subscribers is 150 million, while the average number of internet users is 73 million. These numbers continue to grow annually, potentially expanding e-wallets’ user base. (Quimba et al., 2021)

Weaknesses

  • Cybersecurity Risks

    • Concerns over fraud, phishing scams, unauthorized transactions, and identity theft undermine user trust.

  • Digital Literacy Gaps

    • Limited knowledge of digital tools among certain demographics restricts the potential user base, especially in rural areas.

  • Cost of Access

    • High prices for smartphones and data plans pose a significant barrier for low-income households.

  • Dependence on Internet Connectivity

    • Poor internet infrastructure in rural areas limits access to e-wallet platforms, perpetuating the digital divide.

Opportunities

  • Expanding Features

    • Adding functionalities like microloans, investments, and insurance can make e-wallets more versatile and appealing.

  • Public-Private Partnerships

    • Collaborations to improve internet infrastructure and provide subsidized smartphone distribution can expand market reach.

  • Educational Campaigns

    • Initiatives to enhance digital literacy can empower more users to adopt and benefit from e-wallet services.

  • Targeting Underbanked Populations

    • Leveraging e-wallets to integrate the underbanked into the financial system aligns with government inclusion goals.

  • Sustainability Initiatives

    • Refurbished smartphone distribution programs and eco-friendly business models can appeal to socially conscious users.

  • Broadening Merchant Partnerships

    • Expanding partnerships with more companies and merchants can boost e-wallet adoption. In China, Alipay has successfully grown its merchant network, partnering with retailers, online marketplaces and public services such as utilities, transportation, and government services (Alipay, 2023).

  • Introduction of Innovative Features

    • Incorporating new features such as bill payments, money transfers, and investment options can help attract a broader user base. Valencia and Layman (2021) assert that technological innovation has a favorable and substantial influence on consumer fulfillment. Additionally, innovations like face recognition technology enhance the ease of use for ShopeePay e-wallets during online shopping, which not only improves user satisfaction but also fosters greater customer loyalty.

  • Global Market Penetration

    • E-wallets can expand globally, especially in regions with high mobile penetration. Team (2024) states that digital wallets enable cross-border transactions, allowing businesses to access international markets and increase sales and revenue.

  • Promoting Digital Financial Inclusion

    • E-wallets promote financial inclusion by providing banking services to marginalized areas. Digital services reach businesses and individuals in remote regions or without access to traditional banks, benefiting countries like Bangladesh and Ghana with limited banking access (Blaney, 2024).

Threats

  • Competition Among Providers

    • The rapidly growing e-wallet market may lead to oversaturation and price wars, reducing profitability.

  • Regulatory Challenges

    • Stricter data protection and cybersecurity laws may increase compliance costs and complexity.

  • Technological Barriers

    • Uneven technological infrastructure could slow down adoption rates, particularly in remote areas.

  • Economic Inequalities

    • Persistent income disparities may prevent significant portions of the population from adopting e-wallets.

  • User Resistance

    • Security incidents and technical glitches may result in users reverting to traditional payment methods.

  • Consumer Trust and Security Issues

    • Security breaches or negative publicity can damage consumer trust and slow e-wallet adoption. Sharp (2024) argues that a data breach can erode trust, causing customers to switch to more reputable competitors. A survey by Ortiz et al. (2023) found many Filipinos still prefer cash. E-wallet providers need to improve security and features to encourage in-store usage and support a "cashless" society.

  • Cybersecurity Vulnerabilities

    • E-wallets, despite security features, remain vulnerable to cyberattacks, fraud, and identity theft, eroding user trust. A poll by Appdome found 76.7% of Filipinos used mobile apps more often, with e-wallet adoption rising, but many would stop using apps if security was in doubt. Cruz (2024) stressed that brands, especially banks and e-wallets, must implement strong security measures to maintain customer trust and success.

  • Regulatory and Legal Hurdles

    • According to Odronia (2024), a major challenge for the Philippines' digital payment systems is creating a legal structure that keeps pace with rapid technological advancement. The Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) overseas payment systems, ensuring security and efficiency, but rapid technological advancements often outpace regulatory updates.

  • Integration with Existing Systems

    • Integrating e-wallets with banking, point-of-sale, and payment systems poses challenges such as compatibility, data synchronization, and secure communication (Mohmmed et al., 2024).
